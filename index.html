ROOM 01 · THE MAN

A scholar between
two mathematical eras.

Isaac Barrow was an English mathematician, classical scholar, and theologian. He became Cambridge’s first Lucasian Professor of Mathematics and taught Isaac Newton.

1630Born in London
1663Lucasian chair created
1677Died in London

Barrow was not simply “Newton’s teacher.” His work on tangents and geometric quadrature helped make the inverse relationship between differentiation and integration visible before calculus had its modern notation.

ROOM 02 · A LIFE IN MOTION

From restless student
to Cambridge professor.

1630

His early schooling was difficult, but he later mastered languages, philosophy, geometry, and optics.

1655–59

A scholar abroad

He travelled through Europe and the Mediterranean, even surviving a pirate attack.

1663

The Lucasian chair

He became Cambridge’s first Lucasian Professor of Mathematics.

1669

Newton succeeds him

Barrow resigned, and the young Isaac Newton took the position.

1670

Geometry in print

Lectiones Geometricae collected his work on tangents and areas.

ROOM 05 · BARROW & NEWTON

The mentor who
recognized the future.

Newton attended Barrow’s lectures and discussed mathematics with him privately. Barrow encouraged his talent and stepped away from the Lucasian chair in 1669, allowing Newton to take the position.

1663 BARROW First Lucasian Professor
THE HANDOFF

Barrow did not create modern calculus alone—but he helped make Newton’s breakthrough possible.
